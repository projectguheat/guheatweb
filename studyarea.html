Baguio City

Baguio City is the Philippines’ summer capital and is one of the 180 creative city worldwide hailed by United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO). The city which is the center of business, commerce and education in Northern Luzon is approximately 250 kilometers north of Manila, situated in the Province of Benguet. Of the City’s total land area, only 467.5 ha (8.1%) still have old growths of pine while 1, 137.5 ha (19.8%) have production pine stands and 122.5 ha (2.1%) are brush lands. The remaining area of 4,021.2 ha making up 70% comprises the developed portion. The city is comprised of 129 barangays and has a population of 345,366 as of 2015. All the barangays are energized thru a system managed by the Benguet Electric Cooperative (BENECO) and has a road network of 315.9 kilometers for its circulation system. Majority or 90% of these roads are paved with either concrete or asphalt. On average, the temperature ranges from 15 to 23 °C. Baguio is 8 degrees cooler on the average than any place in the lowlands of the country. The coldest months in Baguio City are usually November, December, January, and February, while the hottest months are March, April, and May. Also, the city receives the biggest amount of rainfall in the country, twice the volume of rainfall as compared to Manila.

Cebu City

Cebu City is located in the middle of the eastern coast of the island province of Cebu, in Region VII. The seat of the provincial government is located in Cebu City, while the city itself retains political independence from the provincial government. It is comprised of 80 barangays subdivide into two congressional districts. Barangays closer to the coast are generally smaller, more densely populated, and primarily residential or industrial in nature, and barangays towards the northwest are generally larger and less economically developed. It is the fifth most populated city in the Philippines, and as of the 2015 census, has a population of 922,611 people, the most populous barangays being the primarily residential barangays of Guadalupe, Lahug, and Tisa. The city is host to many business process outsourcing companies and is an important hub of tourism, trade, and industry in the region. The Port of Cebu is the second largest port in the Philippines, and due to the city’s geographic location in the center of the country, it has become a gateway for travel in the Visayas and Mindanao.

Davao City

Davao City is a highly-urbanized city located in the southeastern part of Mindanao. It is the capital city of the Davao Region and is the largest city in the Philippines in terms of land area with 244,000 hectares. The third-most populous city in the Philippines is Davao City after Quezon City and Manila. When the City of Davao began on March 1, 1937, its population was listed at about 68,000. By 1940 it increased to 98,000. In 1970, Davao City’s population size was 392,473 and by 2015, it had quadrupled to 1,632,991 (CPDO, 2015).A large part of Davao City is mountainous, characterized by extensive mountain ranges with uneven distribution of highlands and lowlands. Compared with other parts of the Philippines in which there is a distinct hot and wet season, Davao City experiences the privilege of a mild tropical climate where the days are always sunny and followed by nights of rain. It is outside the typhoon belt and lacks major seasonal variations. Its annual average temperatures range from 25.0 to 32.8 °C with the hottest occurring during the month of April, and the coldest occurring on January based on observations in 2018 (PAG-ASA, 2018)

Iloilo City

Iloilo City is the capital of the province of Iloilo located in the southeastern part of Panay Island. The city covers a land area of approximately 78.4 square kilometers with a population of 461,841 as of 2016. This highly urbanized city is the regional center of Western Visayas. As of 2015, it is composed of 180 barangays and seven administrative districts: Arevalo, City Proper, Jaro, La Paz, Lapuz, Mandurriao, and Molo. Based on the recent Comprehensive Land Use Plan of Iloilo City, majority of the city’s land area is occupied by residential zones (57.35%), followed by commercial zones (8.21%), and park and open spaces (5.50%). The city has a monsoonal climate consisting of dry and wet seasons with the hottest months from March to May and its coldest months are usually from December to February. According to a 2010 climatological data from the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration (PAG-ASA), the average temperature of the city throughout the year is 27.8 °C. Within the city lie three major water bodies namely: Jaro River, Batiano River, and Iloilo River.

Mandaue City

Mandaue City is situated in the eastern coastal plains of Cebu province located at 10 9’ 40.34” N, 123 5’ 28.16” E with an average elevation of 16.8 meters above sea level. It has a land area of 3,487 hectares and is comprised of twenty-seven barangays. It is categorized as a Highly Urbanized City (HUC) and recognised as the most industrialized city in Metro Cebu (Castillo et. al, 2018). It is known as the focal point of manufacturing in the Philippines and is considered as the industrial hub in Cebu province hosting about 20,000 industrial and commercial establishments. Furthermore, the Mandaue local government unit (LGU) plans that by 2020 “Mandaue will be the Home of High Value Manufacturing Serving the Global Market”. Despite being a highly industrialized city, the Mandaue LGU envisions Mandaue City as a green city in the future.

Quezon City

Quezon City is a highly urbanized city located in the northern part of Metro Manila. It is composed of 6 districts with a total of 142 barangays and as of 2015, a population of 2,936,116. It has a land area of 161.12 sq. km and an average elevation of 92m. The average maximum and minimum temperature are 35°C and 30.5°C respectively. The months with the hottest temperatures recorded are from March – May.

Zamboanga City

Zamboanga City, located on the southwestern tip of the Zamboanga Peninsula, is an independent, chartered and a 1st class highly urbanized area in the region. It is bounded by Zamboanga del Norte and Zamboanga del Sur on the north, Sulu Sea on the west, Moro Gulf on the east and on the south by the Basilan Strait and Celebes Sea The city is the 6th most populous having a recorded population of 861,799 last 2015 census and 3rd largest city in the Philippines with a total land area of 1,483.3849 square kilometers. The city has two congressional districts with a total of 98 barangays, of which 54 are coastal barangays, including 10 island-barangays, and has a total coastline length of 301 kilometers. It has more than 50 rivers and creeks, and has a total waterfront length of 1,396.66 kilometers including 33 islands and isletsThe commercial areas in the city are heavily concentrated well-within the 7-kilometer radius from the City center. Most of the establishments are businesses engaged in food, services, wholesale, and retail. According to PSA classification, the urbanization level for the year 2015 is 86.60%, though it’s the City’s relatively modest density indicates that there is much area for development and expansion of the city. Climate in the city falls under the Type III based on the Modified Coronas Classification. The city is located outside the typhoon belt and rarely experience typhoons but is located within one of Mindanao’s high-risk zones and is considered one of the most vulnerable cities to the effects of climate change. The Manila Observatory (2016) projected an increase in temperature by 2.1℃ in 2050 and in contrast a decrease in precipitation rate at -0.6% by 2025 and -5.2% by 2050.
